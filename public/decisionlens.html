DecisionLens

Explainable & Fair Algorithmic Decision System.

XAI Decision System

Plain Language Explanations & Counterfactuals

DecisionLens is an XAI system built for critical domains including credit scoring, graduate admissions, and criminal justice risk.

Each module natively computes decisions while taking extra steps to explain those decisions in plain language. It calculates counterfactual recourse — providing exact pathways or specific metric changes needed to alter an adverse outcome.

Finally, DecisionLens systematically audits for demographic bias across protected groups to ensure fair decision-making environments.

Core Audit Modules

Credit Scoring

Evaluates lending logic. Provides clear reasoning when a loan is denied and actionable steps (counterfactuals) a candidate can take to gain approval in future applications.

Graduate Admissions

Provides standardized rubrics and self-service explainability so applicants can see specifically where they fell short compared to cohort baselines, eliminating the 'black box' of university rejections.

Criminal Justice Risk

The Judicial module replicates the ProPublica COMPAS bias finding and visualizes Chouldechova's (2017) fairness impossibility theorem — demonstrating analytically that algorithmic bias optimization is fundamentally a governance and tradeoff problem, not merely a math error.
