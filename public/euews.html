EUEWS Dashboard

Election Unrest Early Warning System

Real-Time NLP Pipeline

How It Works

Data Ingestion: The system continuously ingests a stream of tweets, public Facebook comments, and TikTok text via social media APIs. It filters content using targeted hashtags (e.g., #GhanaVotes, #ECGhana).

Linguistic Engine: Rather than relying on generic English sentiment models, the core AI is powered by a fine-tuned AfriBERTa model. It actively parses complex syntactic structures in Pidgin, Hausa, Ga, Ewe, and Akan. This allows the system to accurately assess intent in phrases like "We no go gree" rather than flagging them indirectly or missing nuance altogether.

Risk Scoring & Stir Analysis: Posts are categorized into Low (Opinion), Medium (Aggressive), and High (Incitement). The overall "Stir" is calculated via a hybrid score blending the AI's toxicity probability with the virality of the content (measuring retweets and rapid shares).

System Architecture & Applications

How It Is Built

The backend operates on a Python FastAPI and Celery/Redis stack for asynchronous processing of massive social streams. The NLP model (AfriBERTa) is trained on manually annotated datasets to ensure high precision. Finally, a reasoning layer powered by a smaller LLM (e.g., Llama-3-8B) summarizes exactly why a cluster of posts is causing unrest.

Where It Can Be Used

Electoral Commissions: To monitor and counter digital misinformation that delegitimizes the electoral process in real-time.

Security Agencies: To deploy resources efficiently by identifying geographical heatmaps of rising tension before physical escalation occurs.

Advanced Visualization

The dashboard provides stakeholders with a "Timeline of Tension", mapping out chronological risk spikes as Election Day approaches. Additionally, an interactive heat map highlights regional clusters of inflammatory vocabulary, translating raw NLP output into immediate, actionable intelligence.
